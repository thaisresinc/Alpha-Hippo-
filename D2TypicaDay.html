⏰ 7:30AM BALI TIME
Team Check-in — Start Together
WHAT HAPPENS
Every day starts with a team check-in at 7:30am. Log in on time, dashboard open, ready to go. This is your daily alignment with Luke and the team — energy, focus, any updates before the first sprint.
WHY IT MATTERS
The first 20 minutes set the tone for the whole day. Showing up ready at 7:30 is a habit that separates consistent performers from inconsistent ones. If you're late, WhatsApp your TL immediately.
🏆 TOP PERFORMER TIP
Have your CRM open and dialer loaded before 7:30. Know how many new leads are in the queue before Luke even asks. Walking in prepared is the first signal of a productive day.
CLOSE ✕
🔥
⏰ 7:50AM – 10:00AM
Sprint 1 — New Leads & Priority Callbacks
WHAT HAPPENS
Work new dialer leads and priority callbacks — your freshest, highest-value contacts. These are D2D reschedules loaded overnight. Work through the dialer as it pushes leads to you, and hit your priority CRM callbacks first.
WHY IT MATTERS
Recency is everything. A lead that was knocked yesterday is warmer today than it will be tomorrow. Sprint 1 is your highest-energy window — it meets your highest-quality leads. Don't waste it on anything lower priority.
⚡ THE LOGIC
"New leads go first, every time. These customers had a face-to-face conversation recently — they're expecting a call. Your opener lands differently when it's fresh in their mind."
CLOSE ✕
☕
⏰ 10:00AM — 15 MINUTES
Break — Reset & Recharge
WHAT HAPPENS
A hard 15-minute break after Sprint 1. Step away from the dialer completely. Hydrate, move, reset. Don't use this time to check CRM — it's a genuine break.
WHY IT MATTERS
Calling for 2 hours straight depletes your energy faster than you notice. A 15-minute reset makes Sprint 2 significantly more effective than if you pushed straight through. Performance is a marathon, not a sprint — except when it's literally a sprint.
🧠 THE MINDSET
The next customer on Sprint 2 deserves your A-game, not whatever's left after 2 hours straight. Protect this break — it's part of the system, not a reward.
CLOSE ✕
📊
⏰ 10:15AM
Sprint 1 Review — Learn Before You Dial Again
WHAT HAPPENS
Brief team check-in with Luke. How did Sprint 1 go? Bookings, contact rate, anything that came up. This is a fast feedback loop — not a long debrief. What worked, what didn't, what to adjust for Sprint 2.
WHY IT MATTERS
The best setters don't wait for their monthly 1-1 to get feedback. They adjust every sprint. If your opener isn't landing, 10:15 is when you find out — not at the end of the day when it's too late to fix it.
📅 COME PREPARED
Know your numbers before the review. How many contacts? How many attempts? Any bookings? Luke will ask — have the answer. The habit of tracking your own data is what separates good setters from great ones.
CLOSE ✕
💪
⏰ 10:30AM – 12:30PM
Sprint 2 — Continue Lead Callbacks
WHAT HAPPENS
Continue working newer lead callbacks from the dialer — the leads you've already attempted and need another touch. Keep the momentum from Sprint 1 going. If you set callbacks in Sprint 1, work them here.
WHY IT MATTERS
Most bookings don't happen on the first attempt. Sprint 2 is where persistence pays off. You've already warmed up your voice and your mindset — use that momentum to follow through on the leads you've already invested in.
🎯 COACHING NOTE
"If the customer can't book within 7 days, set a callback for yourself and let them know you'll follow up personally a few days before their requested time. We can't see availability more than 7 days out."
CLOSE ✕
🍽️
⏰ 12:30PM – 1:00PM
Lunch — 30 Minutes, No Exceptions
WHAT HAPPENS
A hard 30-minute lunch break. Dialer closed, CRM closed. Eat properly, step away from the screen, give your brain a genuine reset before the final push.
WHY IT MATTERS
Sprint 3 is the last push of the day — Luke loads fresh leads and it's an intense final block. You need fuel for it. Setters who skip lunch notice the difference in their Sprint 3 energy within a few weeks.
⏰ TIME IT
Back at your desk by 1:00pm for the Sprint 2 review. Not 1:05. Not "just finishing my lunch." The team runs on time — and it starts with you.
CLOSE ✕
🎯
⏰ 1:00PM – 1:15PM
Sprint 2 Review — Last Push Setup
WHAT HAPPENS
Quick debrief on Sprint 2 + setup for Sprint 3. How's the day tracking? What's working? Luke uses this moment to load new leads for Sprint 3 and set the focus for the final block.
WHY IT MATTERS
Sprint 3 is your final push — it deserves a clear target, not a vague "keep going." This 15 minutes makes sure you walk into the last block knowing exactly what you're aiming for.
🔥 SET YOUR TARGET
Before Sprint 3 starts, know your number. How many bookings do you have today? What would make today a great day? Set the target in your head and dial with that in mind.
CLOSE ✕
🚀
⏰ 1:15PM – 3:15PM
Sprint 3 — The Final Push
WHAT HAPPENS
Luke loads fresh leads for Sprint 3. Work new lead cycling, after-hours callbacks, and continue the dialer. This is the last concentrated calling block of the day — treat it like the first.
WHY IT MATTERS
Many setters fade in Sprint 3. The best ones don't. Afternoons catch people at home — families together, people more relaxed. After-hours callbacks can be your highest contact rate of the day if you show up with the same energy as Sprint 1.
🧠 THE MINDSET SHIFT
"Depending on contact % and lead volume, Luke may direct you to switch to training data during Sprint 3. Follow the TL's direction — they're watching the numbers in real time and making the call that benefits the team."
CLOSE ✕
🏁
⏰ 3:15PM – 3:30PM
Call Reviews & Wrap — Leave Nothing Behind
WHAT HAPPENS
Individual call reviews — select your best or most interesting call from the day and send it to Luke or the group chat for feedback. Then close the day: CRM notes updated, callbacks created, dialer dispositioned.
WHY IT MATTERS
Call reviews are how you improve faster than anyone who doesn't do them. One call shared = one pattern identified. And a clean wrap means you walk in tomorrow knowing exactly where you stand — not spending 20 minutes catching up.
✅ THE END-OF-DAY CHECKLIST
Call shared for review ✓  |  CRM notes updated ✓  |  Callbacks created ✓  |  Dialer dispositioned ✓. Four things. Every. Single. Day. Finish at 3:30.

THE NON-NEGOTIABLES
Three Rules That Hold It Together
01
Be there at 7:30
Not 7:32. Not "just logging in." Dashboard open, dialer ready, energy on. The team check-in sets the tone — and your preparation before it sets yours.
02
Follow the sprint order
New leads first, callbacks second, training data when directed. The order isn't arbitrary — it's designed to match your energy to the value of your leads. Ignore it and your results become unpredictable.
03
Share a call. Every day.
One call sent to Luke or the group chat at wrap-up. Not when you feel ready. Every single day. The setters who share calls improve faster than anyone. That's not a coincidence.
