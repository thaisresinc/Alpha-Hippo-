What Happens Next

This process exists to help you recover, not to punish you. Every step is a chance to course-correct before things get harder to fix.

The Minimum Standard
Know the numbers before you dial

Performance is tracked every day against three KPIs. Falling below any one of these triggers the process below — not as a punishment, but as an early signal that something needs to change.

25%
Min Net Sit Rate
The floor. Below this and the downstream pipeline breaks down.
50%
Confirm Rate
Of booked appointments confirmed by the confirmation team.
50%
Sit Rate
Of confirmed appointments that actually sit with the specialist.
🎯
WHY THIS PROCESS EXISTS
Performance issues don't fix themselves — they compound. The earlier this is caught, the easier it is to fix. This four-step process is designed to give you every possible tool to turn it around before formal action becomes necessary. It's not a trap. It's a recovery plan.
The Process
Four steps — in order
1
Reviewed daily
KPI Review & Coaching
The first response to falling below minimum is not a warning — it's a conversation. Luke reviews the KPI data with you, identifies exactly where the gap is, and provides direct coaching on how to close it.
What gets reviewed: Net sit rate, confirm rate, sit rate — all three, against your actual call data.
What Luke provides: Specific coaching on the behaviour driving the gap — not generic advice.
Your job: Come prepared. Know your numbers. Be ready to talk about what's happening on your calls.
2
If no improvement within 2 weeks
Formal Review
If daily coaching hasn't moved the numbers after two weeks, the situation moves to a formal review. This is the point where the conversation becomes documented and structured.
What triggers it: No measurable improvement in KPIs after two weeks of Step 1 coaching.
What it means: The issue is now formally on record. Both parties understand the standard and the gap.
The goal: Clarity — not blame. You know exactly what needs to change and why it matters.
3
Zoom call — TL & starter present
Data Review & Action Plan
A structured Zoom call with Luke where past data is reviewed and a tangible two-week plan is agreed. This is the most important step — it produces something concrete.
What's reviewed: Past weeks' data in full — calls, sit rates, Big 5 scores, patterns.
What's set: Clear targets for the next two weeks. Not vague — specific and measurable.
The outcome: An agreed action plan that both you and Luke commit to. Written down. Followed up.
Your job: Come with calls ready to review and a clear view of where you think the gaps are.
4
Ongoing — alongside steps 2 & 3
Intensive Support
Alongside the formal process, intensive one-on-one support is activated. This is the full toolkit — everything available to help you turn the numbers around.
1-1 coaching: Dedicated session time with Luke focused entirely on your calls and your gaps.
Call shadowing: Luke listens to your live calls and provides real-time feedback on what to adjust.
Group reviews: Your calls reviewed with the team — you hear from multiple perspectives at once.
Out-of-hours practice: Roleplay, self-development, and practice calls outside shift hours. This is where the real improvement happens.
Important Context
What this process is — and isn't
❌ What this is NOT
This is not a path to termination by default. It's not designed to catch you out. It doesn't mean you've failed. It doesn't mean Luke has given up on you. A single bad week doesn't trigger this — it's a pattern that needs addressing.
✅ What this IS
This is a structured recovery framework. It's the clearest possible signal that something needs to change — with a plan, support, and time to do it. Most people who go through Step 1 never reach Step 3. The process works when you engage with it.
❌ The wrong response
Going quiet. Hoping it fixes itself. Avoiding the coaching conversations. Not sharing calls for review. The data doesn't lie — and ignoring feedback only makes the gap harder to close.
✅ The right response
Show up to every coaching conversation prepared. Share calls proactively. Ask specific questions. Treat out-of-hours practice as an investment, not a penalty. The setters who recover fastest are the ones who lean in hardest.
"
Nobody wants to go through this process. But if you find yourself below minimum, the worst thing you can do is wait. Step 1 exists so Step 3 doesn't have to. Engage early, share your calls, and use the support that's available to you. That's what it's there for.
